SMILE Lab RampUp
===================================

The goal of this guide is to develop a principled and practical understanding of how to formulate and answer scientific questions using neural data. Rather than focusing solely on technical execution, these exercises are designed to cultivate the underlying logic of experimental reasoning: how to translate a conceptual question about brain function into a testable hypothesis, identify the appropriate analytical tool, and properly implement it to meaningfully address that question.

A central objective is to build fluency in the common techniques used in the lab, including data preprocessing, visualization, and dimensionality reduction. Equally important is learning when and why to use these methods. It is critical that one understands the assumptions and limitations of the techniques they employ. By engaging directly with real neural datasets collected in the lab, these assignments emphasize the iterative nature of scientific analysis: exploring data, refining questions, validating results, and critically evaluating outputs.

These assignments are structured to accommodate trainees at different stages. The graduate student track emphasizes foundational concepts in experimental design, neural data analysis, and core lab techniques. It is designed to ensure a strong conceptual and methodological base. As a result, more experienced postdocs may choose to skip or move quickly through sections that review familiar material.

.. note:: These assignments are designed to provide a strong foundation in how to approach scientific questions using neural data, but they are not intended to be comprehensive or exhaustive. They introduce core concepts and commonly used techniques in the lab to establish a shared baseline of understanding. However, if a research project relies heavily on a specific method or analytical framework, trainees are expected to develop deeper expertise beyond what is covered here. Mastery of specialized techniques often requires additional reading, hands-on practice, and engagement with the broader literature.

.. note::

   This project is under active development.
