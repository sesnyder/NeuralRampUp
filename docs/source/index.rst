SMILE Lab RampUp
===================================

The goal of this guide is to develop a principled and practical understanding of how to formulate and answer scientific questions using neural data. These exercises are designed to cultivate the underlying logic of experimental reasoning: how to translate a conceptual question about brain function into a testable hypothesis, identify the appropriate analytical tool, and properly implement it to meaningfully address that question. A central objective is to build fluency in the common techniques used in the lab, including data preprocessing, the most used analytical techniques, and data visualization. Equally important is learning when and why to use each methods. These exercises are built on a raw dataset that I collected of a monkey performing a basic reaching task.

These assignments are structured to accommodate trainees at different stages. Undergraduates and early graduate students may choose to skip over some of the more difficult implementations for now, while new postdocs may find their time better spent going deeper on techniques than I do here.

Finally, these assignments are designed to provide a strong foundation in how to approach scientific questions using neural data, but are not intended to be comprehensive or exhaustive by any means. I introduce core concepts and commonly used techniques in the lab to establish a shared baseline of understanding. However, if your specific research project relies heavily on a specific method or analytical framework, you are expected to develop deeper expertise beyond what is covered here. This will require additional reading, hands-on practice, and engagement with the broader literature.

.. note::
